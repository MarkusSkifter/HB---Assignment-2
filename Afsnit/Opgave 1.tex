\section{Question 1: Weighted Household Utility}\label{sec:q1}

\subsection*{Model Change}

In the baseline model from, the household utility function is an equal-weighted sum of individuals utilities:
%
\begin{equation}\label{eq:baseline_util}
    U(c_t, h_{1,t}, h_{2,t}) = 2\frac{(c_t/2)^{1+\eta}}{1+\eta} - \rho_1 \frac{h_{1,t}^{1+\gamma}}{1+\gamma} - \rho_2 \frac{h_{2,t}^{1+\gamma}}{1+\gamma}
\end{equation}
%
We then introduce the weight $\mu \in [0,1]$ on member 1 and $(1-\mu)$ on member 2. 
\newline
The individual utilities are:
%
\begin{equation}
    u_j(c_t, h_{j,t}) = \frac{(c_t/2)^{1+\eta}}{1+\eta} - \rho_j \frac{h_{j,t}^{1+\gamma}}{1+\gamma}, \quad j \in \{1,2\}
\end{equation}
%
The new weighted household utility is the convex combination:
%
\begin{equation}\label{eq:weighted_util}
    U(c_t, h_{1,t}, h_{2,t}) = \mu \, u_1(c_t, h_{1,t}) + (1-\mu) \, u_2(c_t, h_{2,t})
\end{equation}
%
Since $\mu + (1-\mu) = 1$, expanding gives:
%
\begin{equation}
    U = \frac{(c_t/2)^{1+\eta}}{1+\eta} - \mu \, \rho_1 \frac{h_{1,t}^{1+\gamma}}{1+\gamma} - (1-\mu) \, \rho_2 \frac{h_{2,t}^{1+\gamma}}{1+\gamma}
\end{equation}
%
The only change relative to the baseline is that the labor-disutility weights are now $\mu$ and $(1-\mu)$ rather than $1$ and $1$.

\subsection*{Implementation}

We add a single parameter \texttt{par.mu = 0.5} to the model class. In the \texttt{util()} function, the disutility weights become \texttt{par.mu} for member 1 and \texttt{1-par.mu} for member 2, and the consumption term is \texttt{(cons/2)**(1+eta)/(1+eta)} with no leading factor of 2.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Note on the comparison:} Although the weighted utility at $\mu = 0.5$ is a factor-$\tfrac{1}{2}$ rescaling of the baseline (see Question~2), utility is ordinal --- the argmax is invariant to any positive scaling. The optimizer therefore chooses identical hours, which is why the maximum difference below is \emph{exactly} zero.
\medskip

Furthermore a check for differences is made where we see that:

\begin{align*}
    \max|\Delta h1|=0.00e+00 \\
    \max|\Delta h2|=0.00e+00
\end{align*}
